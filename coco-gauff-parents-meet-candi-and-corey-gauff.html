Coco Gauff Career Summary

Gauff began playing tennis at an early age and quickly progressed through the ranks. In 2019, she earned a wildcard entry into the main draw at the French Open.

She continued to make a huge impact on the world stage at Wimbledon, where she defeated Venus Williams in the first round and became the youngest player to reach the round of 16 since 1991.

Not only is she successful on the court, but she is also known for her outgoing personality and her commitment to using her platform to advocate for social and political issues such as racial inequality. She has been a vocal supporter of the Black Lives Matter movement.

Gauff’s talent, determination, and magnetic personality make her a rising star in the world of professional tennis. Her career statistics include a 162-84 singles record, 1 singles title, and career prize money of $5,826,957.

Additionally, she reached the highest ranking of World No. 30. Her awards include ITF Junior World Champion (2018), WTA Newcomer of the Year (2019), and WTA Most Improved Player of the Year (2019).

Her best Grand Slam results are the 4th round of the US Open 2020, the French Open 2020, the Australian Open 2020, and the 3rd round of Wimbledon 2019. With her impressive results and continued progress, Coco Gauff is expected to be a dominant force in women’s tennis in the coming years.

Who Are Coco Gauff’s Parents?

Coco Gauff was born on 13th March 2004 to Corey Gauff and Candi Gauff. She grew up in Atlanta, Georgia. Her parents were both athletes and this has been a big influence in her life. Coco also has two siblings namely Codey and Cameron.

Gauff’s parents gave her the best education and training to help her reach the stage she is in today. For her education, she was home-schooled in order for her to get time to train.

She started playing tennis at the age of six. Later on, she and her family moved back to Delray Beach, Florida, so that Coco could get proper training from a renowned coach.

Who Is Coco Gauff’s Father?

Coco’s father is called Corey Gauff. He played college basketball at Georgia State University and later worked as a healthcare executive.

Who Is Coco Gauff’s Mother?

Coco’s mother was called Candi Gauff and she was a track and field athlete and an educator at Florida State University.
